Biography

I am a fifth-year PhD candidate in Computer Science at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), supervised by Prof. Irwin King, Prof. Joseph J. Y. Sung and Prof. Hao Chen. Prior to my doctoral studies at CUHK, I was a visiting and research student at the Johns Hopkins University (JHU) under the supervision of Prof. Philipp Koehn, and a visiting student at the University of Cambridge. I obtained my B.Eng. of Computer Science degree from Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT), where I have been closely working with Prof. Weinan Zhang.

My research focuses on gigapixel image understanding and multimodal learning for computational pathology. I develop data-efficient methods for whole-slide image representation, pathology foundation model feature adaptation/evaluation, visual compression, and WSI-genomics fusion, using computational pathology as a testbed for weak supervision, scarce labels, domain shift, and clinically grounded evaluation.

Before my PhD, I worked on low-resource word alignment and bilingual lexicon induction in NLP/MT. This earlier experience connects to my broader interest in representation learning and alignment across languages, images, and heterogeneous modalities.

I welcome collaborations on computational pathology, gigapixel image understanding, multimodal learning, and biomedical AI. Feel free to reach out by email.
